# Example:

Founding and general overview:   
Bank of America traces its lineage to the establishment of the Bank of Italy in San Francisco on October 17, 1904 under founder Amadeo P. Giannini, and later adopted the Bank of America name. Today it operates internationally and is one of the largest banking institutions in the United States. 

Products and services:   
Bank of America offers a full-range of financial services to individuals, small- and middle-market businesses, large corporations, institutions and governments. This includes retail banking (checking, savings, mortgages, credit cards), small business banking, wealth management and private banking, corporate and investment banking, asset management, and risk-management solutions. For example, its Consumer Banking segment provides services via thousands of bank branches, ATMs, online and mobile banking; it also originates mortgages, home-equity loans, auto loans and other credit products. 

Mission / purpose beyond profit:   
Bank of America promotes a purpose beyond simply making profit: it asks the question 'What would you like the power to do?' as part of its brand positioning. Its stated purpose is 'to help make financial lives better through the power of every connection.' The bank emphasises 'Responsible Growth' — meaning growth that is client-focused, risk-aware, sustainable and that delivers value for clients, shareholders and communities. In other words, the company frames its mission as helping customers, communities and employees succeed financially, while also addressing societal challenges. 

Brand, tagline and logo:   
Bank of America’s tagline/brand question is: 'What would you like the power to do?' The company’s visual identity includes a stylised 'Flagscape' logo — a red and blue flag-like emblem of bands representing the American flag motif, updated recently in a brand refresh to appear more modern and confident. Other slogans cited include 'Bank of Opportunity.' The brand emphasises reliability, modern digital access, personal empowerment and the idea of being a partner in financial goals. 

Target customers and problems solved:   
Bank of America serves a broad set of customers: everyday individual consumers and households; small and middle-market businesses; large corporations and institutional clients; and government entities. For individual customers it solves problems such as: accessible banking channels (branches, ATMs, digital/mobile banking), credit for major purchases (home, auto), wealth-management and planning services, making financial lives simpler and more secure. For businesses and institutions it solves problems such as managing finances, raising capital, risk-management, investment banking and asset management.

In essence, Bank of America aims to help customers achieve their financial goals (buying a home, growing a business, managing wealth) by providing the infrastructure, products and advice that reduce friction, improve access and empower financial decision-making.
