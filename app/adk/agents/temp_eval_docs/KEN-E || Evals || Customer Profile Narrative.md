# Customer Profile Narrative

# Evaluation Factors:

* Does it include a bulleted list of problems that are painful for this type of persona?  
* Does it describe what motivates this type of persona to make a purchase to solve this problem?  
* Does it identify a list of communication channels that are effective for reaching this persona with messages about a product?  
* Does it contain a bulleted list of demographic characteristics about the persona, such as age, geography and gender?  
* Does it contain a bulleted list of psychographic characteristics about the persona, such as values, culture and interests?  
* Does the content include only complete thoughts that can be understood without any other context?  
* Is the character length between 2,000 and 6,000?

# Example:

Demographics:  
\- Age: 25-40 years old  
\- Gender: All genders; no single gender focus  
\- Education: Bachelor’s degree or higher, likely professional/white-collar job (e.g., tech, consulting, marketing)  
\- Location: Urban or suburban U.S. markets, in growth metro areas; comfortable with mobile/digital services  
\- Household income/purchasing power: Upper-middle class ($75K-$150K+), often dual-income household, with discretionary income  
\- Race/ethnicity/cultural background: Diverse; not narrowly targeted  
\- Other person-level details: Single or married (often without children yet or young children), early-to-mid career stage, time-constrained but tech-savvy

Psychographics:  
\- Values: Convenience, speed, digital-first experiences; values financial control, investment awareness; cares about innovation, user experience  
\- Culture: Busy lifestyle; blends work and personal life via mobile; values mobility and flexibility  
\- Activities: Uses mobile apps for everything, travels, socialises, invests online, may have side-hustle or freelance work  
\- Interests: Fintech, financial wellness, investing (self-directed or via advisor), digital banking, peer networks, lifestyle experiences  
\- Opinions: Believes banking should be frictionless, expects digital tools, dislikes legacy banking pains (slow branch queues, disjointed apps)  
\- Hobbies: Fitness, travel, podcasts/webinars on productivity, online communities, early adopter of technology

Needs / Jobs-to-be-done:  
\- A comprehensive banking platform accessible via mobile with fast onboarding and integrated services (checking, savings, cards, investing).  
\- A financial partner that helps streamline everyday money management (pay-bills, transfer funds), while also enabling investing/growth.  
\- Real-time insights, alerts and personalised recommendations to manage cash-flow, savings and investment goals.

Pain Points:  
\- Frustration with legacy banks: difficult UIs, slow service, limited integration between banking \+ investing.  
\- Lack of time: balancing job, lifestyle, personal finances means digital convenience is essential.  
\- Overwhelmed by investing: wants simpler tools or advisor support rather than navigating alone.  
\- Disconnected pie-cobbling of multiple financial apps/services rather than one ecosystem.

Goals:  
\- Consolidate banking and investing with a trusted institution.  
\- Grow savings/investments efficiently while managing lifestyle and budget.  
\- Maintain mobility and access—banking anytime, anywhere.  
\- Build wealth for future milestones (home purchase, family, early retirement).

Motivations:  
\- Digital convenience and control of finances in one place.  
\- Desire to leverage expert resources \+ innovative tools for investing.  
\- Sense of empowerment over money, rather than just reacting.  
\- Reputation—being with a strong brand that supports digital lifestyle and financial wellness.

Buying behaviours & channels:  
\- Discovery: Social media, fintech blogs/podcasts, app store rankings, peer recommendations.  
\- Consideration: Compares mobile apps, digital features, account fees, integration with investing, rewards.  
\- Conversion: Opens account online via smartphone, transfers funds, sets up mobile alerts.  
\- Loyalty: Engages regularly via app, uses investing tools, refers friends; responds to push notifications, digital campaigns.  
\- Channel preference: Mobile app first, website second, branch visits rarely; email and in-app messaging for communication.

Preferred communication channels:  
\- Push notifications / mobile alerts  
\- App in-context messages  
\- Email promotions for investing/wealth planning  
\- Social media (LinkedIn, Instagram) for brand engagement

Exclusion criteria:  
\- Customers who strongly prefer branch-only service and minimal digital-use.   
\- Those who only need basic banking and are uninterested in investing or growth.
